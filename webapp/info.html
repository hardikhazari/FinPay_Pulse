Understanding RFM Analysis

What is RFM?

RFM stands for Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value. It is a proven marketing analysis tool used to identify a company's best clients based on their spending habits. Each customer is assigned a score from 1 to 5 for each of these three metrics.

🕒 Recency (R-Score)

How recently did the customer make a transaction?

Customers who have transacted recently are more likely to buy again compared to those who haven't interacted with the platform in months. A score of 5 means they transacted very recently, while a 1 means they have been inactive for a long time.

🔄 Frequency (F-Score)

How often does the customer transact?

This measures the total number of transactions over a given period. Frequent customers are highly engaged and loyal to your platform. A score of 5 indicates extremely high engagement, while a 1 means they rarely use the app.

💰 Monetary (M-Score)

How much money does the customer spend?

This calculates the total monetary value of a customer's transactions. Big spenders are vital to business revenue. A score of 5 represents your highest spenders, while a 1 represents customers who make very small or zero-value transactions.

Customer Segments

By combining the R, F, and M scores, the system automatically classifies every user into actionable business segments:

  • Champions Bought recently, buy often, and spend the most. Reward them to keep them loyal.
  • Loyal Spend good money and transact frequently. Upsell higher value products to them.
  • At-Risk Spent big money and purchased often, but haven't returned recently. Send personalized win-back emails.
  • New Users Bought recently, but not often. Provide excellent onboarding to increase their frequency.
  • Dormant Below average recency, frequency, and monetary values. Will be lost if not reactivated.
